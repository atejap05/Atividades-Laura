Challenge Section — Use Everything!

Read carefully

Today is Friday at my school! I wake up at 7 o'clock and get dressed quickly. At 8 o'clock, I am waiting at the bus stop. I arrive at school at 8:30. My sister is in the library reading a book. In the classroom, we are studying English. Our teacher is helping us with grammar. At 12 o'clock, everyone is eating lunch in the cafeteria. My brother is playing soccer on the playground. At 3 o'clock, school ends. I am very happy because it is Friday! My mother is picking me up at 3:30. We are going home together. I will do my homework at 4 o'clock, but first, I am going to have a snack. My family is eating dinner at 6 o'clock. What a great day!

Scoring Guide

Multiple Choice (Q1-24, 31): 1 point each = 16 points
Written Answers (Q4-5, 9-10, 14-15, 19-20, 25-27, 32-35): 1-3 points depending on complexity (0=no attempt, 1=partial/simple, 2=correct, 3=excellent with details)
Total Maximum: 35-40 points
Grading: 34-40 = Excellent (A) | 28-33 = Good (B) | 22-27 = Satisfactory (C) | Below 22 = Needs Review

Notes for Teachers

  • Present Continuous: This is often new for elementary students. Accept minor errors if the -ing form is attempted correctly.
  • Written Answers: Look for effort and communication rather than perfect grammar. Encourage full sentences but accept simple phrases if time is limited.
  • Time Telling: Accept both "3 o'clock" and "It is 3 o'clock" formats.
  • Flexible Grading: For questions 10, 20, 26, 35 (personal responses), focus on vocabulary use and sentence structure rather than singular "correct" answers.
  • Extensions: For advanced students, ask them to write a longer paragraph combining multiple topics (e.g., "My typical Wednesday at school").
